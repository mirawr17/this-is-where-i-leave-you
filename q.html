If You Ever Wonder, Know This Was Love

A Letter From Mira

I know you won't read this.

This is the only way I know how to express what I feel.

Maybe you’ll think it’s cringe, but to me, it means everything.

Please, for the last time, read this.

After that, I’ll disappear from your life forever.

If You Ever Wonder, Know This Was Love

July 2025

Dear Qomar,

I don't know how to write to you without crying.

It all began on the 1st of January, 2025, when our paths quietly crossed. From that day onward, I became the happiest person in the world. I don't even know why. Maybe because even when sadness crept in, I had you, my friend. My light.

At first, I only wanted to be your friend. That's all I thought I could ever be. But life, as always, had other plans.

I fell in love with you.

I denied it, fiercely. No, it wasn't love, I told myself. Just infatuation. Just a fleeting feeling. But no, Mira. You don't behave like that for something so shallow. You don't lose sleep, write poems, and feel this deeply for something that isn't real. And slowly, painfully. I admitted it to myself.

Yes. I liked you.
Yes. I loved you.

Even though we never met, what a fool I was.

But I planned to. As soon as I finished my diploma and came back to Johor, I planned to meet you. You didn't know that. Because once again, life had other plans.

I could've been angry at myself, at fate. But instead, I wrote poems.

And when I wrote those poems about you, I couldn't stop listening to Falling in Love by Cigarettes After Sex. Yes, every single day. That song became my quiet confession. My silent way of loving you from a distance. Every time it played, I thought of you. Every time it played, I missed you.

Falling in Love
Cigarettes After Sex

I thought, Maybe if I write what I feel now, I'll understand it better in the future. I reread those poems again and again... and yes, I saw it clearly. I was in love with you.

"Poor Mira," I whispered to myself.

You never knew how much those little words of yours meant to me. Especially the day you said, "If I were to find someone pun, it's you." I knew you didn't mean it the way I hoped. I knew I shouldn't fall for it.
But dumb, hopeless me, I still did.

♡

When February came, I felt the distance between us grow.
And when you admitted that you were the one pulling away, I wanted to scream: Why did you get close to me only to walk away?

I wasn’t just angry. I was heartbroken. Because I don’t think it was ever about the fact that we’re not from the same country. I think you just came into my life to fill a hole in your heart, and when I wasn’t exciting anymore… you left.

It was disappointment. Deep, hollow disappointment.
Because I never expected you to love me. I never hoped for a romantic fairytale. All I ever wanted was for you to stay. To be my friend. But even that… was too much to ask.

And that was the day I thought Leomatch was a curse. A damn cruel joke. I hated it. I hated everything.
Before you, I was self-aware. I could see the red flags. I was cautious. I would leave at the first sign of pain.

But with you… I stayed. I was stupid. I was soft. I was everything I swore I’d never be.
You never knew that I talked about you every day to my friend. Every. Single. Day.

I told her how I missed you, how I couldn’t sleep at night. How much I missed your voice, your face, the way you’d say things only you would say.
I told her I didn’t understand why you couldn’t just be my friend.

I told her, “This isn’t me. I don’t act like this. I’ve never let anyone make me feel this way.”
But with you… I broke all my own rules. I was strict with my heart before. I respected myself enough to walk away. But with you… I stayed.

I know for you, it was probably just a casual moment. Something you’d say or do with anyone. But for me, it was different.

♡

And after we stopped talking, I found myself listening to The Cut That Always Bleeds by Conan Gray. Over and over again. It was like the song understood what I couldn't say out loud. Every lyric felt like the wound you left behind.

The Cut That Always Bleeds
Conan Gray

So I reread our old conversations. I looked at your picture, because that's all I had left of you. That's all I will ever have.

♡

When you told me about your past, I didn’t judge. I didn’t question. I just wanted to understand. I wanted to know you, not just the version you showed to the world, but the version you hid. The one that hurts. The one that needed someone.
And when you finally apologized, I couldn’t even reply. Because I was hurting too much. You said there was no guarantee that we could become anything. But I never wanted a guarantee. I never asked to be anything.

I just wanted to stay in your life.
And even that… was too much.

When I started my degree, You were always in my mind. I thought, If he was still here, I could share this with him. I could tell him about my day. But no. You’re not here. And you won’t be.
So I stopped waiting. I won’t reach out again. I won’t hope for a reply. Because I finally understand: I was in denial for so long. But not anymore.

This is it, Qomar. This is my final goodbye.
I will leave you alone, forever. I will not disturb your peace. I will not text, or wait, or wish anymore.

But before I go, I want you to know:

I hope you're happy.
I hope you achieve everything you told me you dreamed of.
I hope you get into the university you wanted.
I hope you start again, and this time, you stay gentle with yourself.
I hope you spend more time with your family; your little sister, your parents.
I hope you ride your bicycle safely, and smile under the sky.
I hope you never stop collecting perfumes that make you feel like you.
I hope you stay close to Allah.
I hope you take care of your heart, your health, and your soul.
I hope you become the person you always wanted to be.

And if one day you think of me... even for a moment, I hope it brings you softness. Not guilt. Not sadness. Just a whisper of memory, like a quiet wind passing through.

I hope you find the one you're looking for.

Take care, Q.
Take care of your dreams, your family, your life.

You will always be a part of my story, even if I no longer belong in yours.

This is my final act of love.
My final goodbye.
And now, I walk away. For real this time.

— Amierah Aizah
